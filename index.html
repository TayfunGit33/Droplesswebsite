How it works

A microscopic layer that water can't grip.

Dropless bonds a hydrophobic film to glass at the molecular level. Water beads up and rolls away — taking soap film, mineral spots, and dirt with it.

i.

Clean the surface

Wipe the glass thoroughly. The cleaner the substrate, the stronger the bond.

ii.

Mist evenly

A few light passes from 20 cm away. The fine mist self-distributes across the surface.

iii.

Wait 10 minutes

The active layer cures and chemically bonds to silica in the glass.

iv.

Buff lightly

A soft microfibre pass removes excess. Done — protection is active.

Common questions

Good questions. Direct answers.

How long does one application really last?

In our 4-person household test, the protected shower glass stayed clear for 47 days with zero cleaning. With monthly light wipes (no harsh chemicals), most surfaces stay protected for 8–12 months.

Will it work on my car windscreen?

Yes — Dropless was tested on automotive glass first. Above ~60 km/h, rain beads roll off the screen without wipers. Reapply every 4–6 months for highway use.

Can I use it on coated or tinted glass?

Yes for tinted glass. For anti-glare or oleophobic factory coatings (phone screens, some monitors) we recommend a small test patch first.

Is it safe around kids and pets?

Once cured (about 30 minutes after application), the bonded layer is inert. Avoid direct spraying near food prep surfaces or inhalation during application — full safety data in the SDS.
